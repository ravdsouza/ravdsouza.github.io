projects & involvements

  • INDUSTRY 4.0 LAB


    The Industry 4.0 Lab is a lab used in Management Engineering. It is designed as a factory and automated from start to finish with the use of PLCs and C# software. It simulates a real factory line by using RFID chips as the objects created and introduces variability by altering the code used to fetch RDIF information.

    In this lab, my role is to develop and debug PLC and C# code to improve the factory's automation and remove bugs for a smoother execution.

  • TRON DAYS


    Tron Days was a two-day event held by during our first term of university where students, in teams of four, applied several skills developed througout the term in various problems. These were designed to integrate our five courses (calculus, linear algenra, digital computation (c++), mechatronics engineering (concepts course), and chemistry. The purpose of these problems were to solve open-ended questions, as seen in the real world, and use our assumptions, engineering judgement, and previous knowledge to come up with an appropriate solution.

    Prior to these two days, we designed a logo that was to represent us during the event using AutoCAD. This was for the development of our CAD skills and helped us collaborate and share ideas to come up with a good design. During the event, we had several problems centered around the movie The Martian where we disproved the probabilty that growing potatoes in that environment was possible, or researching appropriate materials to cover a rip in a high pressure difference wall. These activites helped me develop my problem solving skills, and to collaborate with my teammates to create a successful solution.
